What is Demise?

Demise is an old-school, dungeon-crawling RPG. Your goal is to explore the dungeon to find and defeat the evil therein.

In single player (SP) you can explore the dungeon with a group of up to four characters. These characters are all created by you, and you have complete control over all of them. In multiplayer (MP) you may only play one character at a time.

By exploring and defeating monsters in the dungeon, your character(s) will become stronger and be able to take on more powerful monsters in the depths.

A character may join up to twelve guilds (classes) in Demise and can be a member of more than one guild at a time, but they can only gain experience in their currently active guild. They may switch their active guild in town.

Each guild provides a different set of abilities and/or spells. Some specialise in combat (Warrior) while others focus on learning many spells (Warlock), unlocking chests (Thief) or finding secrets and moving safely though the dungeon (Explorer). Most guilds, have restrictions on what items members can use. The Warlock guild, for example, will not allow their members to wear heavy armour.

Getting Started

My First Character

The first few levels of the dungeon can be very dangerous for new adventurers. Your first character(s) will be quite weak, since they start with few attributes and low hitpoints (HP). Your character will gain HP as they level up, but will not gain attributes. These come in the form of tomes and potions, found in the dungeon.

It is often recommended to start out with a tough fighter character who will have a much easier time in the first few levels than most. It’s this character’s job to explore the first three levels of the dungeon and collect stat boosting tomes and potions for your main character.

Race: Giant

Class: Warrior

Alignment: Neutral

Stats: 14 Str, 10 Int, 10 Wis, 16 Con, 4 Cha, 12 Dex

Once you have collected a around 10 endurance potions or tomes it’s time to make your main character. You can of course keep this character, the giant warrior is a powerful fighter and very durable - more than capable of delving into the deepest depths of the dungeon.

Main Character Options

Here are some example characters that can compliment and add skills to the Giant fighter.

1. Elf Warlock (Artisan 1-30 -> Warlock 1-40+)

Good HP, Good A/D. Blasting/Movement/Resist Spells.

The starting levels in Artisan are well worth it for a spellcaster, providing HP, A/D and early access to items making leveling Warlock much, much easier. Warlocks learn a huge variety of spells to cover almost everything except Charming and Healing.

2. Saris Thief (Artisan 1-30 -> Thief 1-30+)

Average HP, Best Disarming, poor Fighting.

Starting levels in Artisan for HP & A/D. Thief for best disarming. The thieving and fighting skills of the Thief will eventually surpass the Artisan, removing the XP penalty but keeping the bonus HP. Can open magically locked chests.

3. Dwarf Explorer (Artisan 1-30 -> Explorer 1-30+)

Average HP, Best Perception, Movement and Resist Spells.

Again starting in Artisan for HP & A/D. Explorer for best perception and access to resist and movement spells such as Levitation and Teleport.

Skills and Spells

No matter the class you choose, some of the most important skills for a party are:

Required

  • Thieving Skill - Thief, Barbarian, Ninja, Villain
    • teleport traps on chests can be lethal, and corrosion can destroy rare equipment
  • Movement Spells - Explorer, Warlock
    • teleport speeds up leveling, breathing & flight make the dungeon much safer
  • Resist Spells - Sorcerer, Cleric, Warlock, Explorer
    • stoning & drain, acid, fire, magic
  • Perception Skill - Explorer, Ninja, Thief
    • speed up mapping, find hidden doors

Optional / Can Add Later

  • Fighting Skill & Multiple Swings - Warrior, Ninja, Paladin
  • Blasting Spells - Sorcerer, Cleric, Warlock, Villain
  • Healing Spells - Cleric, Magi, Paladin
  • Charming Spells - Magi

Party Size

Going Solo

A single character, in a single guild is the by far the fastest way to level. Less spell points are needed to be used on healing and resistance spells, and a single character needs far fewer tomes/potions than a party of four to maximise their stats. Companions are highly recommended for solo - dragons and spellcasters make the excellent companions to help fight large groups of monsters.

Party Play

Unlike many modern games, in Demise experience is not shared between party members. The only way for a character to gain experience is by damaging and killing monsters. Therefore, a party of four will need four times as much total experience to level than a solo character and ensure each character is contributing to the damage.

Parties can clear most rooms in fewer rounds, making exploration faster and safer. However, the party will often be limited by the Attack/Defence rating of its weakest member since monsters will often attack the weakest party member. Parties can also be clunky to play - targets of healing/buffs need to be selected manually each and every cast, and you’ll need to frequently re-arrange your party order to ensure that each character is gaining enough experience and one isn’t taking all the kills.

The Slot 1 Spellcaster Bug: In versions of Demise previous to Revenge, any damaging spell cast from party slots 2, 3 or 4 (not the left-most slot) will hit significantly fewer monsters than they are supposed to. Usually damaging less than half of the total monsters that it otherwise should. To get around this, the spellcaster needs to be in slot 1 when casting offensive spells. This bug has since been fixed in Revenge of the Tavern Keeper.

Can't it Be Both?

Perhaps the best compromise then, is with a party of two. This has the benefit of twice as much offensive power per round than a solo character, with only minimal party management. When one character dies, the other can carry their body back to the morgue for resurrection rather than wait for rescue. Finally, you have two destinations for Mystic Portal rather than one.

How to Not Die

Maximising Hit Points

Hit points are vital for survival in Demise and large portion of a characters hit points will be gained within the first 30 character levels. So, a little planning at the start will make your character much more survivable in the long term.

This is affected by their current guild and Constitution. Warriors gain the most hits-per-level on average, followed by Artisan and Paladin. Maxing out Constitution at level 1 is highly recommended as this can mean twice as many hit points at level 30. After reaching the MxLv (most guilds around level 25) a character only gains 2hp per level. The extra 100-200 hit points can make quite a difference to survivability in the dungeon, especially early on.

To maximise Hit Points: Choose a martial guild (rather than a spellcaster) for the first character levels and ensure constitution is at maximum for your race at level 1. If playing a spellcaster, or a low HP guild, choose Artisan first then switch guilds after level 30 Artisan is reached. Outlined below:

Maximum HP: Warrior(26) -> Artisan (30) -> Warlock (38) -> main guild
Recommended HP: Artisan (30) -> main guild

If playing solo, you might choose to take the maximum route as HP are invaluable. If playing in a party, where XP gains will be slower, I like to take the recommended route only on any spellcasters (warlock, magi, sorcerer, cleric) and thief these guilds gain few HP/level and have low A/D. To simplify: Take Artisan (30) if not starting as a warrior.

Attack/Defence Rating

Your Attack/Defence rating determines your ability to hit monsters and avoid being hit in return. Since a single hit can instantly kill your character, Attack/Defence ratings are very important. Character level also affects the chance a monster will inflict status effects, such as Poison, Disease, Paralysis or Stone. A high character level and high A/D will make your character nearly untouchable.

Resistances

Resistances directly reduce the damage taken by abilities or spells and lower the chances of being affected by status effects. Since spells and monster abilities (such as fire breath and acid spit) cannot miss, resistances are needed to survive these attacks.

Note only the highest source of a particular resistance is counted. If a character has 50% natural fire resistance and equips an item that grants 30% fire resistance, that character's total fire resistance will be the highest modifier (50%).

Run Away

"The best defense is not being there" - Sensei.

Many monsters can inflict deadly status effects which can deal large amounts of damage, instantly kill or permanently drain attributes and hit points. Particularly Dangerous Monster Types:

  • Slimes: Spit Acid, Corrode
  • Mythical Beasts: Stone
  • Undead: Drain, Paralyse

It is advised to run from these monster types when encountered, until you have high resistance (at least 50%, ideally 90%), and enough defence and character levels to avoid being hit.

Items

Item Restrictions

(Class Restricted) The character must meet the guild and level requirements in the active guild to use or equip that item.

(Not Class Restricted) May be used or equipped as long as the character has met the guild and level requirements previously. Notably, Rings, Amulets and Daggers are Not Class Restricted, meaning that a level 10 Artisan can still equip the Dagger of Stealth if they switch guilds to a level 1 Warlock.

Daggers and Rings are often NCR and great items for multiclassing, giving good weapon damage and spells when switching to low-level guilds.

Companions

Monsters will occasionally ask to join your party. Press ‘J’ to accept the request. Alternatively, you can force monsters to join though Charm spells - learned through the Magi guild or found on items.

Companions are very useful allies in Demise and can be considered mandatory when playing solo. Companions can take hits for you, kill monsters for you or at least keep them occupied if you get paralysed. Many companions have special attacks that can kill large groups of monsters, or instantly kill a single enemy.

You only earn about 25-30% of the experience when companions damage/kill monsters, so companions are best used to ‘finish off’ rooms that your character (or party) isn’t able to deal with in a single combat round. This way you can kill most monsters before they even get a chance to attack.

Tips when using companions:

  1. Keep the party size to 6 or less. Any more than this and companions may ‘accidentally’ attack other party members. Typically, does not happen solo.
  2. Keep companions healed. Just like characters, companions fight better when they have high HP.
  3. Keep companion bind level up. Companions may leave if their bind level drops too low.
  4. Beware of Fright traps! These force companions to leave.
  5. Try not to die. Companions leave when you die.
  6. The spell ‘Life Domination’ permanently binds a companion to you. Their bind level cannot drop.
